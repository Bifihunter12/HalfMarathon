# RACR Quests Tab — Dedicated Side Quests Home

Follow-up to `Runner_SideQuest_Spec.md`: instead of hiding quests behind "Not feeling this run?", give them a real home. Core promise: "Train for your race. Build a stronger body. Keep the journey interesting."

## 1. Quests tab structure

Bottom navigation becomes: **Today · Plan · Quests · Progress · Coach**. "Side Quests" stays the in-product language; "Quests" is the nav label.

Opens with:
- **Active quest(s)** — one main multiweek quest + one optional weekly challenge + individual one-day quests. E.g. "Active Quest: Upper Body Builder — Week 2 of 4 · 3 of 6 workouts completed."
- **Recommended for you** — based on race plan, equipment, fatigue, interests.
- **Build strength** — Strong Start, Strong Runner, Upper Body Builder, Core Armor, Bodyweight Builder, Kettlebell Foundations.
- **Go explore** — Weekend Trail Quest, 1,000 Feet Up, Two-Hour Hike, New Trail Challenge.
- **Quick wins** — 10-Minute Core, Push-Up Ladder, Squat Century, 100 Swings, Carry Challenge.
- **Completed quests** — badges, levels, records, lifetime totals.

## 2. Strength training: every body can do it

Product framing: "Every body can strength train. Every exercise can be scaled." Not everyone can safely do the same squat/push-up/plank/kettlebell move, but almost everyone can do an appropriate variation.

| Movement | Beginner | Standard | Advanced |
|---|---|---|---|
| Squat | Chair squat | Bodyweight squat | Goblet squat |
| Push | Wall push-up | Incline push-up | Floor push-up |
| Pull | Band row | Dumbbell row | Renegade row |
| Hinge | Supported hip hinge | Dumbbell deadlift | Kettlebell swing |
| Core | Dead bug | Side plank | Loaded carry |
| Lunge | Supported split squat | Reverse lunge | Weighted lunge |

The app picks the version by ability, experience, pain, and equipment — never assumes.

## 3. Strength quest collections

**Strong Start** (beginners / inconsistent strength history) — 2x/week, 15-25 min: chair squat, wall/incline push-up, band/dumbbell row, glute bridge, dead bug, farmer carry. Goal: 8 workouts in 4 weeks.

**Upper Body Builder** — 2x/week: push-up progression, dumbbell/band row, overhead press, lat pull/pullover, biceps/triceps, carry, core. Achievement: increase push-ups/rowing resistance/total reps after 4 weeks.

**Core Armor** — 2-3 short sessions/week: dead bug, bird dog, side plank, Pallof press, suitcase carry, glute bridge march. Not "hold a plank until you hate life."

**Strong Runner** (default strength track for runners) — 2x/week: squat/split squat, Romanian deadlift, step-up, calf raise, row, push-up, carry, anti-rotation core.

**Kettlebell Foundations** — technique before challenges: KB deadlift, goblet squat, suitcase carry, supported row, half-kneeling press, swing prep. Unlock "100 Swings" only after finishing this or demonstrating familiarity.

**Bodyweight Builder** (no equipment) — squat variations, push-up progression, split squats, glute bridges, calf raises, planks, bear crawls, wall sits. Likely the strongest free/universal track since everyone can start it.

## 4. Daily challenges, scaled by level

Squat Century, Push-Up Ladder, 100 Kettlebell Swings, 10-Minute Core, Carry 1 Kilometer, 100 Calf Raises, 50 Step-Ups Per Leg, 20-Minute Full-Body Circuit, Upper Body Express, Glute Builder, Strong Ankles and Feet, Runner's Stability Challenge.

The app scales the NUMBER, not just offers one fixed target. Example — **Squat Century**: Start = 50 chair squats, Build = 100 bodyweight squats, Strong = 100 goblet squats, Power = weighted density challenge. All levels earn the same badge; difficulty affects XP, not whether the achievement "counts."

## 5. Quest card design

Shows: name, one-line purpose, duration, frequency, workout time, equipment, "running interference" level (Low/Medium/High), beginner-friendly yes/no. Buttons: Start quest / Preview workouts / Choose difficulty / Add to training calendar.

## 6. Integration with the running plan

Never dump workouts on top of the plan blindly. Check: current run frequency, hard running days, long-run day, existing strength training, available days, session duration, experience level — then place strength intelligently. Avoid heavy lower-body strength immediately before intervals or the long run. Example week:

| Day | Main training | Side quest |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Rest | Upper-body strength |
| Tue | Easy run | — |
| Wed | Intervals | Short core only |
| Thu | Easy run | — |
| Fri | Rest | Full-body strength |
| Sat | Long run | — |
| Sun | Walk/recovery | Mobility |

## 7. Strength onboarding micro-check (first time entering Strength)

"Which statement describes you best?" — New to strength training / Train occasionally / Train regularly / Experienced with barbells or kettlebells. Then: equipment available, preferred workout duration, any pain/limitations, days per week, areas to improve. Under one minute.

## 8. Core product rule

A side quest must provide one of: (1) a measurable achievement, (2) a progressive skill/strength gain, (3) a memorable experience. "Do this 20-minute circuit" is content. "Complete eight strength sessions and improve your push-up level" is a quest.

## Brutally honest take

More valuable than only using challenges as emergency run replacements — strength training helps users feel capable, builds visible progression, supports durability, and gives the app something useful to offer even when someone temporarily hates running. Danger: turning the tab into a cluttered workout library. Keep it goal-based, progressive, integrated with the race calendar.

**Top 3 priorities:** (1) dedicated Quests tab + nav, (2) three starter tracks — Strong Start, Strong Runner, Upper Body Builder, (3) scalable beginner/standard/advanced versions of every movement.

**Start today:** the complete 4-week Strong Runner quest, two 20-30-min workouts/week.

**Stop doing:** treating challenges as isolated repetition targets without progression or context.

**Metric to track:** % of users who start a quest and complete the first two workouts.
